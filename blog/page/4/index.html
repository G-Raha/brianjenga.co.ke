✨ Terence the African: The Freedman Who Shaped World Literature

Born in Carthage around 195 BCE, Publius Terentius Afer (Terence) rose from enslavement to become one of Rome’s greatest playwrights, and one of Africa’s earliest literary geniuses.

Educated under the patronage of Publius Terentius Lucanus, he mastered Greek New Comedy (especially Menander) and transformed it into elegant Latin prose that would influence drama for millennia. 🎭

✍🏿 His six surviving comedies — Andria, Hecyra, Heauton Timorumenos, Eunuchus, Phormio, and Adelphoe — reveal a writer of warmth, wit, and moral depth. Through them, Terence refined Roman theater, introducing subtle characterization, emotional realism, and the immortal humanist creed:

“Homo sum, humani nihil a me alienum puto”

(“I am human; nothing human is alien to me.”) 🌍
