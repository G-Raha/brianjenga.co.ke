Islamic Spain and the Golden Age of Science

History often skips this chapter: from 711 to 1492, Islamic Spain (Al-Andalus) became a beacon of science, culture, and tolerance while much of Europe lingered in the Dark Ages.

  • 📚 Córdoba’s libraries held over 400,000 manuscripts.
  • 🧠 Scholars like Averroes & Al-Zahrawi revolutionized medicine, astronomy, and philosophy.
  • 🌾 Muslim agronomists transformed European diets with irrigation & new crops.
  • 🏛️ The Alhambra & Great Mosque of Córdoba remain architectural marvels.
  • ✍🏽 Knowledge flowed from Córdoba to Paris, seeding the Renaissance.

Yet much of this legacy was erased after 1492. Today, Al-Andalus reminds us that pluralism, science, and cultural exchange spark progress.
