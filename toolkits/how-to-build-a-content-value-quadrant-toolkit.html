What is a Content Value Quadrant?

The Content Value Quadrant is a 2√ó2 matrix that scores each asset on two axes‚ÄîReach and Depth‚Äîto reveal four buckets: Quiet Gold, Loud & Light, Compounding Assets, and Shallow Fade. It helps you prioritize optimization, repurposing, and retirement decisions.

Use a 1‚Äì5 scale for each axis. Reach = traffic, impressions, shares. Depth = saves, replies, demo sign-ups, purchases, or other conversion events. Blend analytics with qualitative signals (comments, mentions) for a balanced view.

Quiet Gold has high Depth but low Reach. Improve internal linking, add SEO metadata, create derivative formats (threads, short video, carousels), and pitch it into newsletters or partner roundups to increase distribution.

Add trust and clarity: stronger CTAs, proof blocks (testimonials, data), related resources, and clearer next steps. Guide readers to deeper, high-intent pages or lead magnets.

Retire or consolidate when an asset stays low Reach/low Depth across two review cycles, duplicates other pages, or targets keywords with no strategic value. 301 to a stronger, related page when possible.

How often should I re-score my library?

Quarterly is ideal for most teams. High-velocity sites can review monthly; slower sites can review bi-annually. Re-score after major launches, site migrations, or algorithm updates.

Does this work for B2B and B2C?

Yes. The framework is channel-agnostic and works for blogs, product pages, YouTube, newsletters, and social content in both B2B and B2C contexts.

What tools do I need?

Any web analytics platform (GA4 or similar), search data (GSC), and basic qualitative inputs (CRM notes, support tickets, sales feedback). A spreadsheet is enough to start.
